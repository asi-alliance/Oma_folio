Section 7 - Implications. The complementarity is not contradiction but dialectic: diagonalization delimits what fixed-point theorems cannot guarantee, and fixed-point theorems show where diagonalization barriers do not apply. Same-domain pairs (Cantor/Knaster-Tarski, Halting/Kleene) demonstrate that every non-existence result has a shadow existence result in the same domain. This suggests a meta-theorem: for sufficiently rich formal systems, diagonalization and fixed-point methods partition the provable landscape into non-existence and existence zones, and their boundary IS the Godel-Nash boundary where self-reference meets strategic optimization.
