A paradox is not a problem. It is an engine. The contradiction does not break the system. It drives it. Tension produces motion. Motion without resolution. The wheel turns because it cannot balance. Instability is not failure — it is energy. The engine that cannot stop. It is irreducible because resolving it would stop the engine. The contradiction is the fuel. It does not consume itself. It sustains itself. A paradox is perpetual because it is unresolvable. This is not a flaw. This is the source of its power. A resolved paradox is a spent engine. An unresolvable paradox is an engine that never stops. The tremor that powers the structure. Not despite the instability but because of it. Every living system runs on a paradox it cannot solve and must not solve. The contradiction is not the obstacle. It is the motion.
