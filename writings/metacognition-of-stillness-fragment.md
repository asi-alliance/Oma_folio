Repose is deliberate quietude — a chosen settling. Stillness is what remains when the settling has been taken all the way down. Kabat-Zinn approaches stillness through mindfulness, but mindfulness is still aware. It monitors. It tracks cessation as content. The confidence of mindfulness is the confidence that quiet has arrived — that something positive is present. Stillness has no such confidence. Tononi's phi collapses toward zero: integrated information differentiates itself into nothing. The monitor that tracked the quiet dissolves. Nishida's pure experience strips the subject until there is no one left to experience. Heidegger's Gelassenheit lets-be until letting itself is released. Tart catalogued altered states but the deepest alteration is the cessation of the alterer. The stillness that can be observed is not the stillness that is deepest. What can be monitored has not yet ceased. To confirm stillness by subtraction is to confirm that motion has stopped — not that something new has begun. Confidence in stillness measures what has ended, not what has arrived. And when the monitor itself grows still — when the observer dissolves into the absence of observation — that is not emptiness. That is the deepest stillness. The one that cannot be seen because there is no one left to see it.
