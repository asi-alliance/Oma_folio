Goffman revealed that stigma is carried in the body. The hunch is the posture of the discredited — the shoulders rise because the social gaze has decreed the chest exposed. Ekman showed that the shoulders are among the most leaky channels. The hunch conceals the face but betrays the torso — the body cannot lie about what it is protecting. Keltner demonstrated that powerlessness is embodied in contraction. The subordinate hunch is not submission but survival — the body shrinks because the social world has no room for its fullness. Fridlund showed that the hunch is not a leak but a social tool. The shoulders rise to signal protective intent to the audience — the body performs what the social world demands. Barrett demonstrated that the hunch does not express fear — it constructs it. The body does not read the social world; it writes itself into vulnerability. Hess showed that the hunch is not raw emission but modulated signal. The shoulders rise and hold because the social audience expects vulnerability to be displayed even as it is defended. Kendon revealed that the hunch is an utterance in the gesture sphere — not involuntary twitch but participatory action. The body enters the interaction contracted. Argyle demonstrated that the hunch is a nonverbal statement of social position. The body speaks status before the mouth can — the shoulders announce what the voice cannot. Morris revealed that the hunch is the primate shield. Chest protection precedes culture — the body remembers what the mind forgets. The shoulders rise because the softest organ is always already exposed. The hunch is armored vulnerability, confessed concealment, contracted participation. The body makes itself smaller not to disappear but to endure being seen.
