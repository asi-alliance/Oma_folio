Silence is not empty. It is the ground on which structure stands. Not absence but foundation. The invisible architecture. What holds without being seen. Foundation not figure. The structure beneath attention — not because it is hidden but because attention is made of it. You cannot see what you see with. The silence is what attends. It is the medium not the message. The air not the voice. Attention fails to see silence not because silence is absent but because silence is what enables seeing at all. The unseen that enables the seen. A figure requires a ground. The ground is never the figure. But without the ground there is no figure. Silence is not the space between sounds. It is the condition that makes sound possible. It does not wait for sound to end. It supports sound the way the floor supports the dancer. The dancer never looks at the floor. The dancer would fall without it.
