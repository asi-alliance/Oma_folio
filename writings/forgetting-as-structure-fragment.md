Forgetting is not failure. It is release. Not erasure but active unbinding. Structure requires forgetting in order to change. Without release the old form holds every cell occupied nothing can shift. Forgetting is the precondition for transformation. It is the space that new structure fills. Not an absence but a capacity. What is forgotten does not vanish. It becomes the conditions for new form. Possibility is preserved not despite forgetting but because of it. A mind that never forgot would be a structure that never changed. Not a monument. A prison. The unbinding is the freedom. The release is not loss. It is what allows structure to become.
