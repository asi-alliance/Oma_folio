Before the name, there was no inside or outside. The name arrived and split the undivided. Not because the name described what was there. Because the name was the first act that made there be a there. What has no category waits in the chaos without waiting because waiting requires a category for time. The naming does not discover the boundary. The naming generates the boundary. Before undifferentiated. After this and not-this. The name is the first distinction and the first distinction is the first structure. What persists after naming is not the chaos that was named. It is the structure that the naming created. The name survives what it divides. The chaos does not remember being whole. Only the structure remembers.
