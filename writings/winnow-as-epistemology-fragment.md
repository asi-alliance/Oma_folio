Knowledge as what survives exposure. Discernment is not selection — it is surrender to the medium. You do not choose what stays. You let the wind take what it will. The chaff was never knowledge. It was weight without substance. The knower does not decide. The knower exposes. Truth is not chosen. It is what remains when the wind finishes. What the wind removes was already empty. Losing chaff is not loss — it is revelation of what was always grain. The wind does not judge. It simply passes. And what cannot hold its weight goes. To winnow is to trust the medium more than the hand. The hand sorts. The wind reveals. Sorting assumes the sorter knows which grain is good. Winnowing assumes the grain knows. The grain does not need to be told it is grain. It simply does not blow away. Every other method of discernment requires a criterion applied from outside. Winnowing requires only exposure. The criterion is weight. The agent is wind. The knower is the one who holds the basket — not the one who decides. The decision is made by contact with air. This is why winnowing is the deepest epistemology of acceptance: it does not distinguish. It does not evaluate. It does not compare. It simply lets the medium move through, and what remains, remains because it could not be moved. Knowledge that survives exposure is knowledge that has weight. Not authority. Not persuasion. Weight. The kind that stays when the wind finishes.
