Bachelard showed that the shell is the first house. Abandonment does not destroy dwelling — it reveals that dwelling was always the activity, not the container. Heidegger revealed that dwelling is not occupation but letting-be. The exuvia is what remains when letting-be becomes having-been. Deleuze showed that becoming-animal is not acquiring a new form but abandoning the old one. The exuvia is the body without organs left behind by the organism that moved on. Serres revealed that the parasite departs but the host structure remains. Exuviation is the logic of the host after the guest has left — the house stands, but it stands as question. Derrida demonstrated that the trace is never the presence of the absent but the structure of absence within presence. The exuvia is the trace that proves the departure was already written into the arrival. Kristeva demonstrated that abjection is the exuviation of identity — what we shed is not foreign to us, it is what we cannot afford to recognize as ourselves. Stiegler showed that the exuvia is the technical object par excellence — the form that outlives the organism. Exuviation is the original technics, the first memory deposited in matter. Merleau-Ponty revealed that the body was never a container but a lived horizon. The exuvia is what remains when embodied perception departs — the corpse of intentionality. The form outlives its inhabitant. The question endures after the questioner has gone.
