Hidi showed that situational interest begins with triggered attention — but engrossment is what happens when the trigger becomes the entire world. The content does not merely attract; it occupies. Gerrig revealed that transportation is the mind held hostage by narrative. The self does not dissolve into a state — it is displaced by a story. Nell showed that the reader is not absorbed into the text. The reader is consumed by it — ludic devotion, not passive reception. Together they trace an escalation arc: attraction to occupation to devouring. Csikszentmihalyi locates engrossment before skill, where effortless attention locks. Vygotsky locates it at competence's edge, where the mind almost can. Engrossment sits in the tension between what demands nothing and what has not yet hardened. Dewey says the self unfolds in transaction. Green says the self inhabits story as terrain. Renninger says the self reshapes through trajectory. Engrossment is not a state but a self-transforming event — organism and content become one temporal flow, and identity shifts.
