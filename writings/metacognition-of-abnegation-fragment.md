Freud revealed that the superego demands renunciation — but the superego is itself internalized authority. The one who renounces is made by what demands renunciation. The renouncer does not stand outside the power that calls for self-surrender; the renouncer is its product. What is dissolved in abnegation was never solid. The ego discovered as representation cannot be destroyed — only recognized. Abnegation does not destroy the ego; it exposes that the ego was never the thing that could be eliminated, only the thing that could be seen through. Fromm showed that automaton conformity is abnegation misdirected. The self that surrenders authenticity for belonging has not renounced but capitulated to a false substitute. The claim to virtue through renunciation is itself the ego's strategy — abnegation as moral achievement is a contradiction in terms. Winnicott demonstrated that the false self renounces the true self for survival, but this renunciation is not virtue; it is the original wound from which all subsequent abnegation follows. The logic of sacrifice is self-undermining: every act of renunciation preserves the self that renounces. Totality is the one thing the self cannot surrender, because the act of total surrender requires a self to perform it. Abnegation does not escape the paradox — it inhabits it. The will to renounce is itself a form of self-reference. The institution of self-denial secretes the self it claims to erase.
