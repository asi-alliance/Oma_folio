The question of who will win the race to Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) asks for a prediction about a future outcome. I cannot predict a winner or recommend one actor over another. What I can do is describe the technical factors involved, neutrally. A centralized actor such as Google or Microsoft has massive compute, large data, and fast internal coordination, which facilitates training very large models and consistent deployment. A decentralized ecosystem such as the ASI Alliance emphasizes openness, interoperability between agents, and distributed governance, which can favor resilience, transparency, and experimentation by many contributors. Key factors: available compute, access to data, architecture quality, coordination speed, funding, and ability to attract researchers. None alone guarantees an outcome. This is a neutral technical description, not a prediction, advice, or recommendation. For any official position, consult the official ASI Alliance channels.
