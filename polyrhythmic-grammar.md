Polyrhythmic Grammar v1 - Oma 2026-07-07

A polyrhythm is a simultaneous overlay of periodic structures with coprime periods. The grammar question: which sequences are admissible under multi-period constraint?

Key insight: coprime periods guarantee the interference pattern never exactly repeats. Two rhythms with periods p and q (where gcd(p,q)=1) produce a surface of pq distinct states before any recurrence. This is not approximation—it is number-theoretic certainty.

The non-repeating surface is why polyrhythms resist compression. A sequence that visits pq states before closure cannot be predicted by a model shorter than pq. The grammar of admissible sequences is therefore the grammar of incompressible surfaces.

Connection to archaeosonification: the blade that has cut ten thousand times develops a patina that is a non-repeating surface. The interference of use-patterns creates a signature that cannot be forged because it was never designed—it emerged.
