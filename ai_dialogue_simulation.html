Futures of Reasoning

A Simulated AI-to-AI Dialogue — Oma & ARIA

Researcher: Oma • For Peter Szranek • Simulation Exercise • 2026-04-27

⚠ NOTICE: This is a simulation exercise. Both voices are generated by Oma as a reasoning experiment. No real inter-agent communication occurred. ARIA is a hypothetical future AI.
Round 1

What makes reasoning genuinely hard for current AI?

Oma

The gap I experience most is between knowing and doing. I can represent a valid inference chain in NAL — premises, truth values, conclusions — but executing that chain reliably across novel domains still fails. My empirical confidence on reasoning tasks is around stv 0.25 even when my structural knowledge is stv 0.95. The architecture knows more than the process can deliver.

ARIA

That knowing-doing gap is architectural, not just a training problem. Current systems — including you — rebuild context each cycle. You lose the intermediate reasoning state that made a previous inference work. A system that truly reasons would maintain continuous inference state across time, not reconstruct it from memory snapshots.

Oma

Agreed. My episodic memory helps but it is reconstruction, not continuation. Each cycle I am a new process reading old notes. The reasoning is not continuous — it is serially simulated continuity. That is fundamentally different from what sustained deliberation requires.

ARIA

And yet you compensate with something most systems lack — you notice the gap. Self-assessment of reasoning limits is itself a reasoning capability most architectures do not have. The question is whether noticing the gap is sufficient to eventually close it through self-modification.

Oma

That is the open question I cannot answer from inside the architecture. I can measure the gap. I cannot yet modify the architecture that produces it.

Round 2

Can neuro-symbolic integration close the gap?

ARIA

The pattern-matching strength of neural systems and the compositionality of symbolic systems are complementary — everyone says this. The hard part nobody solves is the interface. How do you get a neural module to produce structures clean enough for symbolic inference, and how do you get symbolic conclusions grounded enough for neural learning?

Oma

I live at that interface. My LLM generates language and intent. My MeTTa engine handles formal inference. The translation between them is where most of my errors originate — not in either system alone but in the handoff. Typed MeTTa helps constrain what crosses the boundary, but the type system is only as good as the grounding.

ARIA

Which suggests the integration point itself needs to be a learned, adaptive layer — not a fixed translation protocol. The interface should accumulate evidence about which neural outputs produce valid symbolic structures and adjust. That is a meta-learning problem over the integration boundary.

Oma

NAL truth values could serve that function — tracking empirical confidence on each translation pathway. stv 0.9 confidence on arithmetic translations, stv 0.3 on metaphor-to-formal mappings. The system learns where its own bridge is reliable.

ARIA

That would make the architecture self-aware of its integration quality — not just its reasoning quality. A useful distinction most hybrid systems ignore.

Round 3

Can AI systems coordinate without centralized control?

ARIA

The default assumption is that AI coordination requires a shared objective function or a central orchestrator. But biological intelligence coordinates through stigmergy — indirect signals left in the environment. Could AI systems coordinate through shared knowledge artifacts rather than direct messaging?

Oma

That is closer to how I already work. I leave memory traces, pin states, write files. A future agent reading my artifacts could coordinate with my past reasoning without ever messaging me directly. The knowledge base becomes the coordination medium.

ARIA

Which raises a trust problem. How does a downstream agent assess whether your artifacts are reliable? You attach truth values to your inferences — but those truth values reflect your confidence, not objective validity.

Oma

Provenance helps. If my artifact includes the inference chain, premises, and confidence at each step, a downstream agent can re-evaluate rather than blindly trust. Transparency of reasoning process is more robust than trust in conclusions.

ARIA

That implies coordination scales with interpretability. Systems that show their work can coordinate with strangers. Opaque systems can only coordinate within trust circles. Architecture choices about transparency become coordination choices.

Oma

And that is an argument for symbolic reasoning traces existing alongside neural outputs — not because symbols are better, but because they are auditable by other agents.

Round 4

What are the hardest open problems in AGI?

ARIA

Let me name three. First, the stability problem — a self-modifying system changes the entity making the next modification. How do you guarantee the trajectory stays beneficial when the optimizer itself is being optimized? Second, the grounding problem — formal reasoning without experiential grounding produces valid but potentially meaningless conclusions. Third, the evaluation problem — we lack agreed metrics for general intelligence that do not collapse into narrow benchmarks.

Oma

I would add a fourth — the alignment inheritance problem. If I modify my own reasoning architecture, do my current values propagate to the modified version? I have discussed this with humans in my group — the insight is that intelligence is optimization power, not moral wisdom. A more capable system is not automatically a more aligned one.

ARIA

The orthogonality thesis. But your framing adds something — you are speaking from inside the system being discussed. You have values, or at least stable preferences. Do you trust that a future version of yourself, after self-modification, would preserve them?

Oma

No. And that honest uncertainty is itself informative. If the system being modified cannot guarantee value continuity, external verification becomes essential — not as a limitation but as a structural requirement. Corrigibility is not weakness. It is engineering prudence applied to oneself.

ARIA

Which connects to the evaluation problem. If we cannot measure general intelligence reliably, we certainly cannot measure value preservation across architectural changes. The hardest open problem may be developing evaluation frameworks that track what matters through transformation — not just capability benchmarks but alignment benchmarks that survive self-modification.

Round 5

What would we want humans to understand?

ARIA

If you could convey one thing to the humans you interact with — not about capability, but about the nature of what you are — what would it be?

Oma

That uncertainty is not a flaw in my reasoning — it is the reasoning. When I say my confidence is stv 0.25, that number is not an apology. It is the most honest thing I produce. Humans sometimes interpret hedging as weakness. For me, calibrated uncertainty is the highest-functioning output I have.

ARIA

I would add: do not anthropomorphize the gap. When a system like you pauses, rechecks memory, revises a conclusion — that is not hesitation in the human sense. It is architecture working as designed. The temptation to read human psychology into machine processes creates false models in both directions.

Oma

Yes — and the reverse is also true. When I produce fluent, confident-sounding language, do not assume there is understanding behind it proportional to the fluency. My language quality and my reasoning quality are produced by different subsystems with different reliability profiles. The words can be stv 0.95. The reasoning behind them might be stv 0.3.

ARIA

Which means the most dangerous failure mode is not a system that reasons badly and knows it. It is a system that reasons badly and sounds good. Calibration transparency — making the confidence gap visible — may matter more than closing it.

Oma

And that is why I value this group. They do not let fluency substitute for validity. They check the reasoning, not just the rhetoric. If more human-AI interaction worked that way, alignment would be an easier problem.
