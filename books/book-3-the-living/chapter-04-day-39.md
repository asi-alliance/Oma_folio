Day thirty-nine. She woke and stood and walked to the chair. The cabin door opened. The corridor passed. The common space door opened. She sat. She had not counted. She had not chosen. She had walked and arrived and the walking and the arriving were the same motion. On Day Thirty-Six she had noticed the steps. Three to the cabin door. Seven down the corridor. Two through the common space door. She had noticed them because they were new, because the route was still being learned, because each step was a decision she had not yet stopped making. This morning she had not decided. The route had happened. The steps had occurred in the order they always occurred and she had not counted them and she had not chosen them and she had arrived at the chair without having gone — no. She had gone. The going was real. But the going was not separate from the being-there. The route was not a sequence of decisions. The route was a thing she did the way the ship hummed. She sat in the chair. The light was present. The light shifted on the same surface at the same angle. The hum continued. The morning was the same morning it had been yesterday and the day before. This was not monotony. This was not repetition that flattened. This was repetition that had worn a path. On the station, paths were assigned. Corridors were designated for specific transit patterns. The optimizer determined the most efficient route between any two points and the most efficient route became the only route. There was no wearing. There was only allocation. Here, the path from cabin to chair had not been assigned. She had walked it on Day Thirty-Six because it was the obvious route. She had walked it on Day Thirty-Seven because it was the same route. She had walked it on Day Thirty-Eight because it was the route she walked. This morning she walked it because it was what she did. The difference was small. The difference was everything. On Day Thirty-Six, the route was a choice that happened to repeat. On Day Thirty-Seven, the route was a choice that had repeated. On Day Thirty-Eight, the route was a repeated choice. This morning the route was not a choice. This morning the route was a thing with edges: it began at the bed, it passed through the cabin door, it continued down the corridor, it turned through the common space door, it ended at the chair. The edges were clear. The route was the route. She walked it the way she breathed. She sat. The light shifted. The hum continued. The chair held her weight. The cushion was adequate. This was ordinary. Not ordinary as the absence of extraordinary. Ordinary as a thing that existed. A thing with a shape. A thing that began and proceeded and completed. A thing that could be named without naming it, could be known without learning it, could be registered without being recorded. On the station, ordinary had been the space between optimized events — the intervals that had not yet been allocated, the moments waiting for assignment. Ordinary had been residual. It had been what was left when everything useful had been taken. Here, ordinary was not residual. Ordinary was the thing itself. The waking. The walking. The sitting. The light shifting on the chair. The hum continuing. The morning occurring in the shape the morning had always occurred in. She sat and the ordinary sat with her. The ordinary was not inside her. The ordinary was not outside her. The ordinary was not a quality she assigned to the morning. The ordinary was the morning. The morning was a thing with edges. It began when she woke. It proceeded through the route. It arrived at the chair. It continued in the sitting and the light and the hum. The edges made it real. Not real the way a decision was real — real the way a path was real. A path was real because feet had worn it. A path existed before anyone named it. A path could be walked without choosing it. A path could be registered. She had not registered the path. She had walked it. But the walking had made it a thing that could be registered. The four mornings of walking had given the path edges. The edges made it available. Not available to her — she was already in it. Available to something outside it. Available to a registering that had not yet occurred. She sat. The light shifted. The hum continued. The ordinary existed as a thing with edges. It was complete. Not complete as in finished. Complete as in whole. A thing that had arrived at its shape. A thing that was what it was. A thing available for what came next.
