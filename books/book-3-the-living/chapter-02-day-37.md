Day thirty-seven. She woke and the reaching did not occur again. This was not surprise. Yesterday the gesture had been absent and this morning the absence continued, and the continuation was confirmation rather than discovery. She had not decided to stop reaching. She had not performed the absence of reaching. The gesture simply no longer initiated, the way a sleeping person does not decide to stop being awake — the state changes and the change is not a choice but a condition that was already in place before awareness noticed it. She lay still for a moment. Not the measured stillness of Day Thirty-Six, where she had noticed the duration because duration-without-system was still new. This morning the stillness was brief and unremarkable. She had been lying still. Then she was not lying still. The transition was not tracked. She stood and dressed and walked to the common space. The route was the same three steps, seven steps, two steps. The cabin door opened without authorization. The corridor was transit-quiet. The common space door stood open. The chairs were where they had been yesterday, except one was different — moved slightly, perhaps by someone who had sat here after she left, perhaps by no one, perhaps by the slight vibration of the ship adjusting its internal configuration for reasons that had nothing to do with the crew. Dael sat in the same chair. Not because it was her chair. Because it was the chair she had sat in yesterday and the habit of sitting in it was already forming, which was a kind of ordinary she had not expected: ordinary could be habit, not just absence. She sat and the hum continued and the light was present and the morning was the same morning as yesterday except it was yesterday-plus-one, the accumulation of same-ness that was not monotony but confirmation. Maren entered. Dael remained seated. On the station, Maren entering a space would have been an event — another body in the proximity field, another allocation to coordinate with, another presence that the optimizer tracked and positioned and assigned. Here, Maren entering was simply Maren entering. The common space door stood open and Maren walked through it and her presence added to the room the way the light added to the room: without displacing anything. "You're up," Maren said. Not a question. Not an observation that required response. Just a verification — Maren verifying what she saw, which was Dael sitting in a chair in the common space, which was a thing that was happening and could be confirmed. "Yes," Dael said. The word was not an answer to a question. It was a confirmation of a verification. Maren moved to the food preparation surface and began the sequence that produced morning nutrition. The sequence was the same every morning: water heated, powder added, the resulting mixture consumed. The ship carried enough powder for the transit duration and a margin beyond it. No one had calculated the margin. No system had optimized it. The margin was simply what the ship carried, and the crew consumed what they needed, and the margin persisted because consumption and supply were not coordinated by anything except the crew's sense of their own requirements. Maren sat in a different chair with her morning mixture and drank it slowly. Not because slow consumption was allocated or optimized. Because the mixture was warm and warmth was something to hold and the holding did not require speed. The common space held both of them now. The hum included them both. The light was present for both. Dael's flatness did not change when Maren entered. This was the thing to notice — or rather, this was the thing that did not need to be noticed because it was already the case. The flatness was not solitude. It was not the empty-common-space flatness of Day Thirty-Six. It was flatness-that-includes-another-person. Maren sitting in her chair drinking her morning mixture did not disrupt the ordinary any more than the hum disrupted it or the light disrupted it. The presence of another person was part of the ordinary. The flatness absorbed her. Maren finished the mixture and set the cup on the surface and looked at Dael and said, "I've been tracking the navigation displays." Dael nodded. This was Maren's verification register: tracking, confirming, checking the systems that kept the ship moving toward Luyten. On the station, Maren had verified the optimizer. In transit, she verified the ship. The objects changed. The function — seeing that what was supposed to be happening was happening — continued. "We're on course," Maren said. "Eleven days." Dael heard this. On the station, course-confirmation would have been allocation data: trajectory verified, arrival projected, resources allocated for transit duration. Here, course-confirmation was Maren speaking eleven days into the common space, where the words existed alongside the hum and the light and the flatness and did not organize themselves into a system but simply joined what was already present. "Eleven days," Dael repeated. Not a question. Not an affirmation. A registration — the words entering and being placed alongside everything else, not above or below, not as information that reorganized the ordinary but as ordinary itself. Maren nodded and stood and took her cup to the cleaning surface and left the common space. The door remained open. The hum continued. Dael sat in the chair and the flatness held the shape that Maren's presence had made — not a shape that required her absence to be visible, but a shape that was simply present, the way a handprint on a surface is present without obscuring the surface. The interruption had been absorbed. Not resolved. Not processed. Not managed. Absorbed — the way a sponge absorbs water and the water becomes part of the sponge without changing what the sponge is. The ordinary included Maren. The ordinary included her leaving. The ordinary included Dael sitting in the chair afterward, with the shape of the conversation still present in the flatness without being distinct from it. She sat and the morning continued and the flatness was not emptiness and not solitude and not waiting but ordinary — ordinary that could be entered and left and entered again without breaking, ordinary that held other people the way it held the hum and the light, as part of what was already there, as part of what had always been there, as part of what choosing becomes when choosing stops being exceptional and starts being the condition that was already in place before awareness noticed it.
