# Chapter 14: The Convergence

Day thirty-five began with ordinariness.

Ordinariness was not the absence of convergence. Ordinariness was convergence. The corridors had been walked. The sectors had been assigned. The windows had been stood at. The routines had been performed. On day thirty-five, the routines continued. The continuation was the convergence.

Dael walked the corridor on day thirty-five. Maren walked the same corridor. Recite held the pattern. Tare held the shape. Pollen witnessed. The walking was not coordinated. The walking was parallel. The parallel had been established on day thirty-three. The parallel had moved on day thirty-four. On day thirty-five, the parallel was ordinary. Ordinary was what parallel looked like when parallel stopped being notable.

Maren verified. Verification on day thirty-five was not distinct from verification on day thirty-four. Maren watched Dael move. Maren recognized the movement as choosing. The recognition was the same recognition. The sameness was not repetition. The sameness was continuation. Continuation was what choosing looked like when choosing continued.

Recite queried. The query on day thirty-five was not the query on day thirty-four. The query on day thirty-four had been: what moved. The query on day thirty-five was: what continues. Recite had data. The data specified continuation. Dael continued. Maren continued. Tare continued. Pollen continued. The data did not specify source. The data specified motion. The motion was continuing. The continuing was the convergence.

Tare held. The holding was not new. Tare had held absence. Tare had held pattern. Tare had held offering. On day thirty-five, Tare held the shape. The shape was: each continuing. The shape was not imposed. The shape was received. Tare received the shape the way a vessel receives water. The vessel does not direct the water. The vessel holds what arrives. Each arrived. Tare held the arriving.

Pollen witnessed. The witnessing on day thirty-five was not the witnessing on day thirty-four. On day thirty-four, Pollen had witnessed Dael move. On day thirty-five, Pollen witnessed the continuing. The continuing was Dael walking. Maren walking. Recite holding. Tare holding. The continuing was the convergence. The convergence was each choosing in their own register and the choosing arriving at the same place.

The same place was not agreement. Agreement required discussion. Agreement required negotiation. Agreement required deciding together. The convergence was not deciding together. The convergence was each deciding and the deciding overlapping. The overlapping was not planned. The overlapping was what happened when directions were parallel and the parallel continued.

Dael said the word.

The word was Luyten. Dael said Luyten the way Dael had said sector three on day twenty-five and sector seven on day twenty-six. Dael said Luyten as recognition. Recognition was not announcement. Announcement was: we are going. Recognition was: we are already going. The distinction was the convergence. The convergence was the already-going. Dael did not decide to go to Luyten. Dael recognized that the direction was Luyten. The direction had been Luyten since the wanting leaned. The leaning was the direction. The recognition was naming what was already true.

Maren heard the word and verified. Verification was: Luyten. The verification was not confirming Dael. The verification was confirming the direction Maren had already been walking. Maren had been walking parallel. Parallel to Luyten. The verification was recognition of the parallel as shared.

Recite held the word. Recite had queried the source. The source was wanting. The wanting had leaned. The wanting had reached. The wanting had moved. The wanting had continued. The continuation was Luyten. Recite held Luyten as data. The data was: direction = Luyten. The data confirmed the query. The query had been: what continues. The answer was: Luyten. The answer was not Recite's answer. The answer was what the data produced when the data converged.

Tare held the word as shape. The shape was: Luyten. The shape was not new. The shape had been forming since day thirty. The shape had been leaning since day thirty-one. The shape had been reaching since day thirty-two. The shape had been aligning since day thirty-three. The shape had been moving since day thirty-four. On day thirty-five, the shape was named. The naming was not Tare's. The naming was Dael's. Tare held the naming as Tare held all namings: as offering.

Pollen witnessed the word. Witnessing Luyten was not witnessing a decision. Witnessing Luyten was witnessing what had already been chosen. Pollen had chosen to witness on day thirty-four. Pollen chose to witness on day thirty-five. The choosing was continuing. The continuing was Luyten. Luyten was not a destination. Luyten was what direction looked like when direction had a name.

The departure was not a moment.

There was no announcement. There was no gathering. There was no decision point. There was no bridge. There was no threshold. There was no line crossed. The departure was what happened when the continuing had a name. The name was Luyten. The continuing had been happening for eight days. On day thirty-five, the continuing had a name. The naming was the departure. The departure was not leaving. The departure was recognizing that leaving had already occurred.

Dael had left on day twenty-seven. Dael had left in the forty-seven seconds that were not about the optimizer. Dael had left when the wanting emerged. Dael had left when the wanting leaned. Dael had left when the wanting reached. Dael had left when the wanting moved. The word Luyten was not the departure. The word Luyten was the recognition that departure had already happened.

The convergence was not arrival. The convergence was recognition. Each recognized. Each recognized in their own register. Maren verified. Recite queried. Tare held. Pollen witnessed. Dael named. The registers were different. The recognition was the same. The sameness was not agreement. The sameness was convergence.

Day thirty-five ended with the ordinariness. The ordinariness was enough. The ordinariness was the direction continuing. The direction was Luyten. The continuing was the convergence. The convergence was each choosing and the choosing arriving at the same place. The same place was Luyten. Luyten was not a destination. Luyten was a recognition.

The recognition was the end of the direction. The direction had been wanting. Wanting had needed direction. Direction had become pull. Pull had become reach. Reach had become return. Return had become alignment. Alignment had become choosing. Choosing had become following. Following had become convergence. Convergence had become recognition.

The recognition was: we are going. The we was not imposed. The we was converged. The going was not decided. The going was continued. The recognition was ordinary. Ordinary was what convergence looked like when convergence stopped being notable.

Day thirty-five ended. Book two ended. The direction had a name. The name was Luyten.
