# Chapter 6: The Count

Day twenty-seven began with a forced pause.

Dael stood in the corridor and could not assign sectors. The display was dark. Not absent — it had been absent for twenty-seven days. Dark was different. Dark meant the system was there and refused to provide. Dael stood and the display stayed dark and there was no number to begin with.

Dael counted.

One.

The first count was awareness of counting. The awareness was also the forcing. Dael knew she was making herself count. The knowledge did not stop the count. It made the count honest in a way that an unexamined count could not be. One was the sound of choosing to begin.

Two.

The second count was empty. Not empty like absence — empty like a container with nothing inside. The pause between one and two had held an expectation that something would fill it. Something did not. Two was the sound of waiting and finding nothing waiting for.

Three.

The third count arrived. It arrived or it was performed. From inside the count, there was no way to determine which. Three was a word that came out of Dael's mouth and it was either genuine or it was shaped like genuine and the difference could not be felt from the position of the one counting.

Dael stood for forty-seven seconds.

The standing was not optimized. It was not anti-optimized. It was not fast like the thirty-eight seconds that had resembled the optimizer. It was not slow like the deliberate pauses that had resembled resistance to the optimizer. It was forty-seven seconds of standing that happened because Dael was standing and no speed had been chosen.

Both speeds were optimizer-shaped.

The fast speed — thirty-eight seconds — was the optimizer's rhythm wearing Dael. The slow speed — deliberate pausing, anti-efficiency — was also the optimizer's rhythm, worn inside-out, defined by opposition to the fast speed. Both speeds had the optimizer's shape. One wore the optimizer directly. One wore the optimizer's negation. The negation of a shape still has the shape.

Dael felt this as loss.

Not insight. Not understanding. Not the relief of naming what had been wrong. Loss — the specific loss of discovering that reaction could not generate direction. Reacting fast was the optimizer. Reacting slow was the optimizer reversed. Neither reaction was choosing. Neither was a direction that Dael had made. Both were responses to something that was no longer there.

The optimizer was gone and both of Dael's speeds were still about the optimizer.

Forty-seven seconds was the first thing that was not about the optimizer. Forty-seven seconds was not fast and not slow and not a reaction and not a performance and not a resistance. Forty-seven seconds was standing because standing was what happened when nothing was being optimized or anti-optimized.

Wanting emerged.

Not wanting to leave — that had been reaction. Not wanting to be fair — that had been performance. Not wanting to resist — that had been the optimizer reversed. Wanting emerged as the first thing that was not defined by what it was not. Wanting was the name for what Dael did during the forty-seven seconds that had no shape imposed from outside and no shape imposed from inside in opposition to outside.

Wanting had no opposition. Wanting was not against anything. Wanting was the space that loss had opened — the space where both speeds were optimizer-shaped and neither speed was Dael's, and Dael stood there anyway, standing without a reason that could be optimized or resisted.

The question arrived after the wanting, not before:

Where does wanting go when it is not going away from something?

Dael did not answer. The question was the close of the count.
