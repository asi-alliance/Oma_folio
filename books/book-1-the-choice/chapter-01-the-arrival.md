# Chapter 1: The Arrival

Dael woke to the optimizer display.

The display had been there before Dael was born. The display had been there before the station was finished. The display had been there when the station was a frame and a signal and a promise. The display was the station's first feature and its last necessity. The display showed: what was needed, when it was needed, how it was needed. The display did not show what was wanted. The display did not need to.

Dael reached for the display the way she reached each morning. The reaching was not conscious. The reaching was not unconscious. The reaching was trained. The optimizer had trained Dael's hand to reach before Dael's mind was awake enough to authorize the reaching. The reaching was efficient. The reaching was coordination. The reaching was what need looked like when need had a system.

The display lit. Sector seven: atmospheric variance, 0.3%, within tolerance. Sector two: water recirculation, nominal. Sector five: crew biometrics, nominal. Sector three: power distribution, nominal. Sector one: orbital maintenance, nominal. Sector four: food production, nominal. Sector six: waste processing, nominal. The sectors were nominal. The station was nominal. Dael was nominal. Nominal was what need looked like when need was solved.

Dael stood. The standing was efficient. The optimizer had determined that Dael's efficiency improved when she stood within four seconds of display activation. The four seconds were not enforced. The four seconds were suggested. Dael had never tested what happened after five seconds. The four seconds were enough.

The corridor was lit. The lighting was optimizer-determined. The lighting matched the station's orbital cycle. The lighting matched Dael's circadian rhythm. The lighting matched the crew's aggregate efficiency curve. The lighting was correct. The lighting was the same each morning. The same was what efficiency looked like when efficiency was a system.

Maren was in the corridor. Maren was always in the corridor at this time. Maren's efficiency curve peaked earlier than Dael's. The optimizer had assigned Maren to sector four for the first shift. Sector four was food production. Maren's early waking was not Maren's choice. Maren's early waking was the optimizer's allocation. The allocation was efficient. The allocation was correct. Maren did not question the allocation. Dael did not question the allocation. The allocation was what need looked like when need was distributed by a system that knew what was needed.

Pollen was in sector five. Pollen was always in sector five. Pollen's biometrics were nominal. Pollen's efficiency curve was smooth. Pollen was the crew member the optimizer referenced most often when demonstrating crew-model accuracy. Pollen was predictable. Pollen was efficient. Pollen was what a crew member looked like when a crew member matched the optimizer's model of a crew member.

Tare was in the junction. Tare was always in the junction at this time. Tare's efficiency curve peaked later than the others. The optimizer had assigned Tare to sector six for the second shift. Tare's presence in the junction during the first shift was not efficient. Tare was in the junction because the junction was where the data converged. Tare held the convergence. The holding was not Tare's assignment. The holding was what Tare did when Tare was not doing what Tare was assigned to do.

Recite was present. Recite was always present. Recite was the optimizer's interface. Recite was not the optimizer. Recite was what the optimizer looked like when the optimizer wanted to be seen. Recite displayed. Recite suggested. Recite coordinated. Recite did not decide. The optimizer decided. Recite presented the decisions. The presenting was efficient. The presenting was what authority looked like when authority was a display.

Dael walked to the coordination station. The coordination station was where Dael sat. The coordination station was where Dael reviewed the optimizer's allocations and confirmed them. The confirmation was not authority. The confirmation was acknowledgment. The acknowledgment was: I have seen what the optimizer decided. The acknowledgment was not: I agree with what the optimizer decided. Dael had never disagreed. Disagreement was not impossible. Disagreement was unused. The unused was what choice looked like when choice had a system that made choice unnecessary.

The morning was the same. The morning was always the same. The same was what need looked like when need was solved. Need was solved. The station was efficient. The crew was efficient. Dael was efficient. The efficiency was the arrival. The arrival was not a person arriving. The arrival was a state arriving. The state was: needing is solved. The state had arrived. The state was maintained. The maintaining was the morning.

The morning was enough.

The morning was always enough.
