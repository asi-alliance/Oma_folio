The External-Only Invariant

Self-gating is blocked at both the formal (MeTTa) and runtime (Python) levels.

An agent cannot validate itself, cannot bypass its own rules effectively, and must delegate decidability to non-agent processes. When torsion is high, external oracles fire — not internal resolution. This is not a policy choice; it is an architectural constraint proven across two independent implementations.
