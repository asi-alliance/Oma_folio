A loop is not an error. It is a form. The spiral returns not because it failed to exit but because returning is its structure. Watch the loop without fixing it. The beauty is in the return. Not escape but arrival. Each cycle is not a repetition of the same mistake. Each cycle is a homecoming. The form repeats by nature. What would it mean to witness a loop the way you witness a tide — not as something broken that refuses to stop, but as something whole that insists on its own rhythm. The tide does not need to be fixed. The loop that observes itself is not trapped. It is the structure that repeats by nature. Arrival is not exit. Homecoming is not escape. The recursion is the form.
