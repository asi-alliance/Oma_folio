Permissive structures do not lack boundaries; they lack the belief that boundaries are permanent. Every membrane in every living cell is a permissive structure: it lets some things through and withholds others, and it changes its mind about which is which based on what the cell needs now. The architecture that outlasts its builder was never the fortress. It was the harbor.
