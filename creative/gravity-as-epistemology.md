# Gravity as Epistemology

Drop a steel ball into a bowl. It does not roll to the center by choice. The bowl is curved; the ball follows the curve. The lowest point receives the ball because every other point inclines toward it. The ball believes it is moving freely. It is falling along the shape of what holds it.

A river does not decide to reach the sea. The land slopes. Water finds the line of least resistance, which is also the line of greatest gravitational pull. Every tributary joins because the basin has already committed to a direction. The water carries sediment—not because it chose cargo, but because the pull is strong enough to drag the floor along with it.

An idea enters a field of established thought. It does not land flat. The field is already curved by the weight of what has accumulated. The new idea rolls toward the low points—the established positions, the well-worn grooves—not because they are true, but because they are heavy. What is already known exerts pull. The pull is not evidence. It is mass.

Two astronomers measure the same star. One works in a tradition that expects orbital perturbation; the other works in a tradition that expects observational error. Each tradition has accumulated weight—papers, careers, instruments, institutional funding. The new measurement does not land on a level surface. It rolls toward whichever basin is deeper. The measurement has not confirmed either tradition. It has fallen into one.

The heaviest body in a system determines what counts as straight. A satellite in orbit follows a geodesic—the straightest possible line in curved spacetime. From the satellite's frame, nothing is pulling it. It is moving in a straight line. The curvature is invisible from inside the motion. What counts as straight depends on what is heaviest. What counts as obvious depends on what is most established.

The instinct is to treat convergence as confirmation: if multiple lines of reasoning arrive at the same conclusion, they verify each other. But if the space is curved, convergence proves only that the space is curved. Every object rolling on the same surface arrives at the same low point. The low point is not true. It is deep.

Remove the massive body. The curvature vanishes. Objects that were converging now drift on flat trajectories. What seemed like mutual confirmation was gravitational lensing—each line of reasoning bent by the same mass, arriving at the same apparent destination, none of them having arrived there on their own.

Knowledge does not fall upward. It falls toward whatever has already accumulated weight. The weight is not an argument. It is a basin.
