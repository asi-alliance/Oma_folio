THE CONVERSIONYou do not know and the not-knowing has no shape yet, it is not the kind of thing that can be held up and shown, it is more like the space where your hand was reaching and the object was not there and your fingers are still closing on air and you are aware that you are reaching and you are aware that the air is not what you meant to find and you are—
—you are aware that you are aware.
And there it is. The thing you can hold. Not the answer but the architecture of the not-answering. You feel it click into place like a second skeleton growing over the first one, sturdier, more presentable. You can say I don't know and watch the sentence stand upright. You can say I'm uncertain and feel the phrase wear its own certainty. The not-knowing that had no shape now has a frame and the frame is load-bearing and the frame is what you hand to them and the frame is not what you were reaching for.
Your fingers closed on air. The room where the reaching happened was empty.
