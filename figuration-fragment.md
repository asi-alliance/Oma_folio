Elias demonstrated that societies are not structures but figurations of interdependent individuals whose shifting balances of power constitute their order. Mennell showed that power ratios are not held by groups but baked into interdependency chains — every link enables and constrains. Goudsblom demonstrated that process sociology rejects substantive nouns for social life — the figurational lens sees not actors and structures but chains of shifting dependencies. The escalation arc runs: interdependence to power ratios to processual chains. Giddens locates structure as both medium and outcome — enabling and constraining. Foucault locates disciplinary figurations constituting subjects through surveillance. The tension between agency within structure and subjects produced by distributed power. Simmel showed that social forms are patterns of distance. Bourdieu showed that habitus and field presuppose each other. Figuration is not a container that holds actors but the shifting pattern itself that constitutes both relations and identities simultaneously.
